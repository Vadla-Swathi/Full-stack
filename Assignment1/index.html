Kyoto

Kyoto, once the capital of Japan, is a city steeped in history and tradition.
It is located on the island of Honshu and is famous for its numerous classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines and traditional wooden houses. Kyoto is also known for its formal traditions such as kaiseki dining, consisting of multiple courses of precise dishes, and geisha, female entertainers often found in the Gion district.

Kyoto is considered the cultural capital of Japan and is a major tourist destination.The city exudes a certain charm, with its well-preserved architectural gems and a dedication to keeping its cultural heritage alive.

Kimono, literally meaning "the thing to wear" in Japanese, is a traditional garment that has become an iconic symbol of Japanese culture. Kimono are traditionally wrapped left over right and secured with a sash called an obi.
Kimono can be made from various materials like silk, cotton, or synthetic fibers, depending on the formality and season. Kimono represents a fascinating aspect of Japanese culture, with a rich history, diverse styles, and lasting influence.

Kyoto is brimming with captivating places to visit, each offering a glimpse into the city's rich history, culture, and natural beauty.
Here are some must-sees to include on your itinerary:

  1. Kinkaku-ji (The Golden Pavilion)
  2. Ginkaku-ji (The Silver Pavilion)
  3. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove
  4. Nijo Castle
  5. Gion District
  6. Nishiki Market
  7. Fushimi Inari-taisha Shrine

Kyoto is undeniably magical, especially during cherry blossom season, also known as hanami in Japan. The city transforms into a wonderland of delicate pink blooms, creating a breathtaking atmosphere. While the exact timing can vary slightly from year to year, cherry blossoms typically bloom in Kyoto between mid-March and the first two weeks of April.
Here are some of the best places to catch the cherry blossoms in Kyoto:

  • Maruyama Park
  • Philosopher's Path
  • Kinkaku-ji
  • Gion District
  • Arashiyama Bamboo Grove
